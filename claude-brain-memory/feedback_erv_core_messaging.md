When describing ERV (Energy Revolution Ventures) in any outward-facing copy — blurbs, intros, LP emails, briefs — the core messaging MUST be **"energy tech VC"** and the focus is **electrification**. Do NOT use "electrochemistry" as the framing word. Status 2026-07-03: `concepts/erv-boilerplate-blurb.md` now carries this rule correctly, and `concepts/erv-lp-comms-style-guide.md` uses "electrochemistry labs" only as a platform-differentiator fact (fine). Still stale: `concepts/erv-investment-approach.md` (April 2026, PPM-derived "The ERV Way") frames Prosemino as an "electrochemistry incubator" and tags the thesis with `electrochemistry` — treat that page as stale on positioning language.

Canonical tagline: *"an energy tech VC built around one idea: the future is electric."*
Standard model line: *"a global fund with an in-house venture builder, backed by 4,000 sq ft of specialist laboratories and a technical team of 3 professors, 7 PhDs, and 2 engineers."*
Standard scope line: *"we invest at Seed to Series A in foundational technologies enabling electrification."*

**Why:** Marcus's own canonical blurb (reused with LPs repeatedly — see the 5 May 2026 ERV overview email) leads with energy-tech-VC + electrification; "electrochemistry" is too narrow/technical for positioning.

**How to apply:** Use the energy-tech-VC + electrification phrasing in all ERV copy and prefer it over the "electrochemistry" brain pages.
